Recipe Cook and Rating Prediction Model

November 2021
University of California San Diego - Course Project

This project was implemented as a part of the course Recommender Systems and Web Mining. Using the data from Food.com, the first task was to predict whether a user would cook a recipe. Since data only consisted of entries where user had cooked a recipe and given it some rating, for this task I performed negative sampling from the given dataset. Subsequently, I split the data into train and test set and using feature ablation studies I fit a logistic regression model on the test data using inbuilt Python library. The accuracy achieved for this task was ~70%.
The second task was to predict the rating a user would give a recipe. For this task, I fit a latent factor model on the given dataset yielding a Mean Squared Error ~0.8.
The solutions to both the tasks had to be uploaded on Kaggle (Username: Juzer Golwala).

Python, Data Analysis, Machine Learning

Advancing Recommender Systems by Mitigating Shilling Attacks


July 2017 - May 2018
Sardar Patel Institute of Technology - Final Year Project

This was a year long project for which we reviewed existing research in the area of Recommender Systems and various difficulties in building smoothly functioning recommender engines. We shortlisted shilling attacks as our topic of research. After studying various kinds of shilling attacks and their effects on different types of recommender systems, we formulated a statistical model for detection of shilling profiles in recommender systems using collaborative filtering (user-user similarity). Our model improved the accuracy of detection of shilling profiles by ~7% (as of existing research).

Python, Data Analysis and Visualization, Machine Learning

Course Exit System

January 2017 - May 2017
Sardar Patel Institute of Technology - Computer Engineering Department

At the end of every semester, each student had to fill a manual form with multiple choice questions to give feedback about their understanding of the course. I introduced the idea of computerizing this process and built the "Course Exit System". I worked on the back-end and deployment and facilitated its usage during the initial phase. Throughout the life cycle of this project, I presented the working of the system to the Faculty of Computer Engineering Department and ensured their inputs were incorporated in the next iterarion. Post the final presentation, the Faculty members unanimously decided to use the system for better preservation and analysis of responses.

GetHitched

July 2016 - November 2016
Sardar Patel Institute of Technology - Course Project

As a part of the course Web Technologies, we built “GetHitched”, an interactive matrimonial website. Users could register, fill in their preferences, like profiles or reject match requests and share their contact details with other users at their own discretion using the inbuilt mailing system. I worked on the back-end and designed a data driven search algorithm to provide users a list of relevant matches in descending order based on the preferences entered by them.
